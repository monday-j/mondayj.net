# Introduction

I really, really like Red Dead Redemption 2.

It is currently sitting on Metacritic as the 9th best game of all time. It deserves all of this, and more. Rockstar Games have created one of the strongest arguments that games are, in fact, art.

The game stretches close to perfection of its formula. From its visual style to its open-world design, from its main story to its side narratives, this game comes closest to any so far in achieving mastery of its form. 

Video games are still a fledgling art form, and we still struggle to find a balance between functioning game systems and the interactive stories we seek to tell. This game stands to me as a study of the ongoing tug-of-war between systems and stories and this game's attempt to come out the other side. If you want to save yourself some reading, I'll let you know early that it doesn't. Perhaps unintentionally, I think it's in how close this game does get to perfection that new and unforeseen cracks begin to show.

I really, really like Red Dead Redemption 2. But it isn't perfect.
